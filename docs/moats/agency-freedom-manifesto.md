# The Agency Freedom Manifesto

_An open letter to every booking agent in India who's tired of being the middleman no one respects._

---

## 1. The job used to be simple

You knew the artists. You knew the promoters. You made the match. You got paid.

That was the whole business. It worked because information was scarce. Finding a good singer for a sangeet in Jaipur required someone — you — who had spent a decade memorizing who plays what, who shows up on time, who actually sounds like their reel.

## 2. Then the internet happened and the work didn't go away

People said the internet would kill agents. "Everyone can just DM the artist directly." They were wrong about the work, right about the respect.

The work got harder. Now you manage:
- DMs on four platforms
- Quotes in WhatsApp threads that disappear
- Advance payments that bounce
- Riders that arrive the day of the show
- Disputes with no paper trail
- Artists ghosting after the booking

The internet didn't remove the middleman. It removed the middleman's tools.

## 3. The platforms showed up — to replace you, not help you

Every "marketplace" of the last five years had the same pitch to artists: _"Skip the agent. Book directly."_

They raised money telling investors that 15% commission was inefficiency. That agents were friction. That a well-designed app would route demand straight to supply.

None of them worked. Not because the tech was bad, but because trust doesn't route through an app. Someone still has to vouch. Someone still has to pick up the phone at 11pm when the sound engineer doesn't show. That someone is you.

The marketplaces collapsed. The work is still yours. But now the artists have been told for five years that you're the problem.

## 4. What you actually need

Not a marketplace. An operating system.

- A deal vault so you stop losing contracts in email.
- A calendar that talks to every artist's Google Calendar so you stop double-booking.
- Invoices that generate themselves with GST split correctly.
- Escrow so the money is real before the show.
- A dispute process with evidence, not WhatsApp screenshots.
- A reputation card you can send to any client that proves your roster's track record — signed, verifiable, portable.

Nothing exotic. Just the plumbing the job has needed for ten years.

GRID is the operating system for booking agents in India. We don't take your artists. We don't replace you. We make the paperwork disappear so you can spend your time on the only thing that actually matters — knowing who's right for the gig.

Our pricing is ₹15,000/month per agency. Flat. Unlimited artists, unlimited bookings. No per-transaction fee. No commission on your deals. No data mining. No artist poaching.

- We will never sell your client list.
- We will never route your artists to another agent without your permission.
- We will never show your commercial terms to a competitor.
- We will never raise prices on existing customers without 12 months notice.

## 7. What we ask

Give us 30 days. Move one piece of your workflow — just the deal vault, or just the escrow, or just the calendar — onto GRID. Tell us what's broken. We'll fix it that week, because we ship every day.

If it doesn't make your life better, you'll know in a month. If it does, the rest of the stack will follow on its own.

## 8. A bet on the craft

We think the people who will win Indian live entertainment in the next decade are the ones who are already here — agents who've spent ten, twenty years learning the market, artist by artist, city by city. That knowledge is the real moat. Nobody in San Francisco is going to recreate it from a spreadsheet.

We're betting on you. We built GRID so you don't have to choose between being a good agent and being a modern business.

The work is still yours. We just want to help you do it with the dignity it deserves.
