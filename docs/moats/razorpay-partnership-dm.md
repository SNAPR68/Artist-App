## Pre-meeting prep

Before the call, prepare:

1. One-page PDF: GRID current numbers (GMV, agencies, artists, monthly Razorpay volume).
2. Proposed co-brand surfaces in GRID: invoice footer, payout email signature, escrow confirmation page, onboarding screen.
3. A concrete ask for Razorpay: featured partner slot in their merchant directory under "Live Entertainment," a joint blog post, introduction to 3 of their enterprise event-industry clients.
4. Rev share model: standard Route partner terms (no negotiation needed on first call).
5. A proposed 30-day pilot: Razorpay co-brands on 10 GRID agency accounts, we measure reduced support tickets + faster artist payouts, joint decision on rollout.

## What to NOT do

- Don't ask for investment or intro to their fund.
- Don't pitch GRID as "the Razorpay of live entertainment." They'll hate it.
- Don't discuss pricing changes to our Razorpay fees. Not the conversation.
- Don't oversell user numbers — show real MRR + GMV, they'll verify.
- Don't request anything exclusive. Razorpay won't commit to exclusivity, and asking kills the deal.

## Follow-ups

- Day 3 after call: email summary with 3 concrete next steps, dates attached.
- Day 10 if no reply: one-line nudge referencing a mutual.
- Day 30: drop for 60 days, try again with new numbers.

## Success criteria

- **Minimum win**: Listed in Razorpay partner directory under Live Entertainment within 90 days.
- **Medium win**: Joint blog post + 3 warm intros to enterprise merchants in 90 days.
- **Large win**: Co-marketing at Razorpay FTX next year + featured vertical playbook slot.
